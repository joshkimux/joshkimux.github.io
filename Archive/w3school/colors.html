Picking a Theme

Before starting any web development project, we'll need to pick a theme! Creating a core narrative will give direction and pave out our future path.

Made by Chelme

Colors in W3.CSS

W3.CSS uses what we call "material design" as it's foundation for it's overarching theme. It's simple, easy on the eyes, and contemporary; basically, think google. You can view heavier documentation on it here at material.google.com. We're gonna need two colors to fulfill this design- a base color, and an accent. These two colors will help define our core narrative- basically a fancy marketing term for "let's keep all our stuff looking like they belong together." If you wanna learn more about this stuff, read some of these blogs.


Picking the Base

To kick us off, let's imagine what kind of colors match our theme. What are we trying to say? What is our personality? What do we want our end users to feel? Among the colors that you see on this page, what do you think works best?

For our app WINER, let's say we're appealing to a working adult population that's interested in using technology to discover new palletes. I've decided to go with w3-deep-purple, it's mature and has an inquisitive feel to it. This will be our theme's base color; now we need an accent color.


Picking an Accent Color

An accent color is a way to denote important artifacts to your end users and it's a key factor of material design. So how do we pick an accent color? Personally, I like to use material palette; it's a great resource to visually pick out the color combination that works best for you. Try pick out an accent color for yourself here!

For this project I've decided to go with w3-light-green for our accent.
