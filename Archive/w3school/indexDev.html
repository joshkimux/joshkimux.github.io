Building our Index

We'll be making a card-based index which lists WINER's wines all out in an attractive format.


Familiarizing with W3.CSS

If I really wanted to I could throw down a full tutorial on using W3.CSS, but that's not in the scope of making this project work. If you're curious of all the tiny details, you can check out the legit tutorial at w3school's website here.

Anyways here's a quick summary. W3.CSS is like a cascading structure conceptually. Since it's a stylesheet, you'll define divs by giving them classes. These classes will determine what your div will become.

Go ahead and copy generic.html and paste/rename it as index.html! Open it up and scroll down to the main content/body section I've commented out for you. You'll start with your containers by attaching the w3-container class. Then, you'll add on your cards which you can see as your mini-containers of information through w3-card. To give these basic structures a personality you can further add on classes such as w3-deep-purple or add depth to your original card by adding a number i.e. w3-card-4.

As you can see, the padding or spacing seems really off. Generic Wine is super close to the edges and that looks awful! So there's two ways we can fix this problem. We can either A) create a padding rule for a new class in our own stylesheet OR B) use what W3.CSS gives us already. For sake of simplicity let's do the latter. If we pop our content into a w3-container, it will be auto-padded thanks to the rules that already exist for that class. As you can see I've also included an image as well! For organization, let's put our wine image under img->wine->yourwine.jpg. To make it responsive (basically make it fit nicely), go ahead and slap on style = "width: 100%" as well.



Don't like the way that looks? We can also use a horizontal image that's not bound under the w3-container.
